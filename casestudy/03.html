WNYC’s data editor, John Keefe, attended one of Make: magazine’s Maker Faire events and took up a hobby of tinkering with electronics, he was looking for a way to combine WNYC’s participation culture with the emergent maker movement. In the early phase, when Keefe and his team were casting about for ideas, their rough concept involved distributing some kind of custom electronic sensor around a neighborhood, recording some data, and having it sent back to WNYC.
